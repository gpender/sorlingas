Personnel

Managing Director - James Wright, MIMarEST

James Wright joined the United Kingdom Hydrographic Office in Taunton in 2007 to aid the AVCS (Admiralty Vector Chart Service) project. After a year specialising in electronic charting, he undertook the company's IHO accredited cartographic training and became a permanent member of staff. James spent seven years working in various chart branches across the UKHO employing traditional charting methodology to produce both standard (SNC) and electronic (ENC) navigational charts. He would also assess incoming data (surveys, notices to mariners, H-Notes etc) to determine charting action and effect updates to Admiralty products where necessary. In 2015 James moved to the Port of London Authority thus becoming the first specialist cartographer to be employed by a British Port. At the PLA, he worked within the Hydrographic Service to develop and support a folio of fully compliant ENCs as well as bENCs. He now maintains these key safety of navigation products through Sorlingas Data Services. James has previous experience of running a football book publishing business, and as a journalist. A former marathon runner and club tennis player, he now spends his spare time walking in the countryside, sea swimming, travelling with his wife and watching local sport.

Senior Cartographer - Pat Currah

Pat joined the United Kingdom Hydrographic Office 2007 as a Trainee Mapping & Charting Officer, enrolling onto their IHO accredited cartographic training course learning the skills required to produce and maintain standard paper charts. He joined the team responsible for charting Africa and the Mediterranean, quickly building experience across a broad range of data sources. Pat took a secondment into an ENC assessment team responsible for ingesting a global package of ENCs into the UKHO catalogue. Adding ENCs to his standard charting and GIS skills, he then moved into UKHO’s new Hydrographic Database team responsible for data assessment and chart production (paper and ENC) across the UK. Pat left UKHO in 2013 to be nearer family in his native Cornwall, pursuing a career as an Operations Manager firstly in the Wastewater Industry and later within the Seafood Industry. Realising an ambition to return to Cartography, he has formed ‘Currah Mapping & Data Services’, working alongside Sorlingas to produce high quality navigational products and maintenance services.
